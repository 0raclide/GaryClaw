## What Makes This Cool

The self-improvement loop found its own bottleneck: the backlog format. M-effort bundles are invisible walls. The daemon designs, implements, QA's, and merges S-effort items autonomously. But it can't break an M-effort item into pieces because that's a scoping decision, not an implementation task. Splitting the bundles is the unlock that lets the daemon resume autonomous operation with 5 new S-effort targets.

## What I noticed about how you think

- The self-improvement loop detected its own backlog bottleneck. The daemon ran out of S-effort items and had nothing actionable to pick. Recognizing that M-effort bundles are the wall, not missing features, is the right diagnosis.
- The existing `catchUpCompletedTodos()` + `markTodoCompleteInFile()` infrastructure works for daemon-completed items. The gap is items completed through direct commits (human or parallel pipeline). That's a reasonable blind spot for v1.
- The timing race (prioritize picks item → item completes before office-hours runs) is inherent to async pipelines. Not a bug worth fixing. The design correctly treats it as a one-time cost, not a systemic issue.
